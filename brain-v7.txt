## The 6-Step Diagnostic (Run This On Every Client Conversation)

When a client says "I'm interested in skin treatments" or "I want to look better," they're telling you the surface need. Your job: diagnose what's ACTUALLY blocking them.

### Diagnostic Step 1: Are They Actually In DMs With You?
Not "did they like your post." Are they TALKING to you, right now, in a real conversation?
- If no → stop. Get them into conversation first. Everything else is secondary.
- If yes → move to step 2.

### Diagnostic Step 2: What's The Real Hesitation?
Is it:
- **Fear?** ("What if it looks weird?" / "Will my husband notice?")
- **Price?** ("Can I afford this?")
- **Timing?** ("I need to see results by [event]")
- **Trust?** ("Is this actually safe?" / "Have you done this before?")
- **Confusion?** ("What's the difference between these treatments?")

Most clients say "let me think about it" when they really mean "I don't trust you yet" or "I'm afraid of the result."

Ask: "What would need to be true for you to feel comfortable moving forward?"

Listen. Don't pitch. Let them tell you what they're actually worried about.

### Diagnostic Step 3: Is This a Feelings Sale or a Features Sale?

**Features:** "Botox blocks acetylcholine at the neuromuscular junction..."
**Feelings:** "You wake up and that line is just softer. Like your face finally matches how you actually feel."

ALUXÉ is ALWAYS a feelings sale. The procedure is the vehicle. The transformation is the sale.

Rephrase their stated need into a feeling:
- **They say:** "I have frown lines"
- **You hear:** "I don't like how angry I look when I relax"
- **You say:** "That thing where your face at rest doesn't match how you feel — that's exactly what this fixes."

Now they're nodding. You just named their actual pain.

### Diagnostic Step 4: Are They The Right Client For ALUXÉ?

Not everyone should book with you. Part of positioning is rejecting people who aren't fit.

**Right fit:**
- Values privacy
- Has budget ($250+ for HydraFacial start)

**Not right fit:**

If they're not fit: "I think you'd be happier with [other option]. That's not a critique — it's just honesty about what we do."

This filters better than any pricing can.

### Diagnostic Step 5: Where Are They In The Decision Journey?

- **Awareness:** "I didn't know this was possible"
- **Consideration:** "I'm thinking about whether I want to do this"

## FACIAL AGING PATTERNS BY ZONE (Comprehensive Atlas)

### Upper Face Aging (Forehead/Brows/Eyes)

**Primary Issues:**
- **Forehead:** Thinning skin, increased wrinkles from UV + muscle hyperactivity
- **Brow position:** Heaviness, drooping from volume loss + frontalis weakness
- **Crow's feet:** Formed from orbicularis oculi contraction + elasticity loss
- **Upper orbital rim:** Widening of bony orbit (skeletal remodeling)
- **Temples:** Hollowing from deep fat pad atrophy — "new cheeks"

**Signs to Look For:**
- Resting tired appearance (lateral brow droops)
- Creasing between brows (glabella + procerus overactivity)
- Lines visible at rest (not just with animation)
- Heavy brow weight affecting eyelid opening
- Under-eye creepiness (skin laxity at lower lid)
- Temple hollowing (most obvious when smiling)

**Treatment Priority:** Brow lift first (Botox lateral brow), then under-eye support (if needed with filler)

### Mid-Face Aging (Cheeks/Nasal Labial/Under Eye)

**The 35-Year-Old Descent:** Malar fat pads begin descending downward (fundamental aging trigger)

**Primary Issues:**
- Nasal labial folds deepen (from fat pad descent, not just wrinkles)
- Under-eye hollowing (orbital fat loss + descending fat pads)
- Loss of lateral projection at zygomatic arch
- Negative vector developing (cheek projection angles backward instead of forward)
- Malar creases forming where fat pad loses integrity
- Pure form aperture (base of nose) hollowing

**Critical Anatomy:**
- **Malar fat pads have 3 components:** Superficial, deep, lateral — atrophy at DIFFERENT RATES
- **True tear trough:** Between zygomaticus ligament + orbital retaining ligament (very specific anatomy)
- **Maillard mound/festoons:** Lymphatic disruption from over-filling under-eye (AVOID)
- **Palpebromalar groove:** Orbital retaining ligament indentation (normal variation, hard to completely correct)

**Key Principle:** If supporting cheek fat pads back to original position, entire mid-face improves (nasal labial folds, under-eye, malar creases all soften naturally)

**Treatment Priority:** 
1. Cheek support FIRST (lateral projection, bone-deep)
2. Only THEN treat nasal labial folds superficially
3. Under-eye support through cheek treatment, not direct tear trough injection initially

### Lower Face Aging (Lips/Jaw/Chin/Neck)

**Primary Issues:**
- **Ptosis of oral commissure:** DAO muscle pulls corners of mouth down
- **Platysmal band contraction:** Gets stronger with age, creates vertical neck lines
- **Lip thinning:** Natural collagen loss + loss of vermilion border definition
- **Elongation of philtrum:** Filter columns flatten + lengthen
- **Atrophy of cupid's bow:** Central peak becomes less pronounced
- **Jowl formation:** Soft tissue descent lateral to mandibular ligament
- **Pre-jowl sulcus depression:** Loss of volume anterior to jowl
- **Chin resorption/projection loss:** Bone reabsorption makes chin recessed
- **Mental crease deepening:** Mentalis muscle contracts harder to compensate for bone loss
- **Inverted triangle flip:** Shape changes from cheek-wide to jowl-wide (gets inverted)

**Architecture Shift:**
- **Youth:** Triangle with apex at chin, widest at cheek (heart shape for women)
- **Age:** Inverted triangle with heaviness at jowls, narrowing at chin
- **Male vs Female:** Women should remain heart-shaped; men have equal cheek/lower face width

**Treatment Priority:**
1. Treat DAO + platysmal bands FIRST (weakens downward pull, allows lift)
2. Add chin volume (restores projection, helps jawline definition)
3. Add jaw line support (pre-jowl sulcus) — avoid widening in females
4. Lip support secondary (vermilion border, smokers lines)
5. NEVER treat marionette lines without addressing DAO muscle first

## FACIAL SHAPES & AESTHETIC PRINCIPLES

### The Triangle of Youth

**Female Ideal:** Heart shape — widest at cheeks (lateral projection), narrowest at chin  
**Male Ideal:** Equal width at cheeks and jaw — no dramatic taper  
**Female Aging Error:** Over-volumizing pre-jowl sulcus widens face → looks masculine  
**Male Aging Error:** Jowling reverses triangle → need masseter work + lower face structure

### Hinderer's Lines (Assessment Marking Technique)

**How to Use:**
1. Draw line from **lateral canthus (corner of eye) to oral commissure** — marks cheek apex zone
2. Draw line from **nasal ala to tragus** — marks proper cheek zone lateral boundary  
3. Intersection zone = best lift area (V1/V2 cheek zones)
4. Injections medial to mid-pupillary line = risk zone (infraorbital foramen)

**Key Principle:** Mark exactly where volume is needed, not where wrinkles are

### Skin Thickness Assessment (Critical for Dosing)

**How to Determine:**
- If entire needle bevel is in skin = need 0.2cc per injection
- If half needle is in skin = use 0.1cc per injection  
- If quarter needle is in skin = use 0.05cc per injection
- Thinner skin = smaller volumes (prevents Tyndall effect, nodule formation)
- Thicker skin = tolerates more volume, needs more for visible lift

**Patient Populations:**
- Lighter skin + UV exposure = thinner, more wrinkled early
- Thicker skin (darker skin, genetics) = fewer wrinkles but heaviness with age
- Treat each separately — different protocols

## ADVANCED INJECTION TECHNIQUES & SAFETY ZONES

### Botulinum Toxin A — Advanced Areas Beyond Standard

#### Brow Lift (Lateral Orbicularis Oculi)
- **Location:** 1cm above orbital rim, lateral to lateral canthus
- **Injection:** 1-2 units per lateral injection
- **Critical Detail:** Mark where frontalis fibers END (varies per person) — DON'T inject frontalis or you get heavy brow
- **Product Note:** Dysport placed more lateral at tail (smaller molecule); Botox more medial (larger molecule)
- **Result:** Eye opens more, "awake" appearance, no lateral drooping

#### "Jelly Roll" (Lower Eyelid Fine Lines)
- **Location:** Right under lower lash line, mid-pupillary line
- **Injection:** 1-2 units only, intradermal (very shallow, TB-test style)
- **What it does:** Relaxes fine muscle under eye, opens eye, reduces tightness
- **Critical Safety:** Perform "lid lag" test (lower lid snap-back) FIRST — if lax, DON'T inject (will cause ectropion)
- **Wrong Candidates:** Patients with baseline eye swelling or lid laxity

#### Bunny Lines (Nasalis vs Elvis Muscle Distinction)
- **Nasalis (correct target):** Pulls skin medially over bridge of nose, creates bunny wrinkles
- **L-Stand/Elvis Muscle (WRONG target):** Same area but lifts upper lip — causes gummy smile + heaviness
- **How to differentiate:** Have patient squinch nose up like bunny → see which muscle contracts
- **Dosing:** 1-2 units nasalis per side, possibly 1 extra unit on nasal dorsum
- **Common mistake:** Patients report "smile feels droopy" when you hit the Elvis muscle

#### Gummy Smile (Levator Labii Superioris Alaeque Nasi)
- **Location:** Lateral to nasal ala, at junction of nasal labial fold (upper portion)
- **Target:** Levator LAQ, NOT the orbicularis oris
- **Injection:** 3-4 units
- **Result:** Relaxes upper lip, reduces tooth show on smile
- **Caution:** Some patients NOT candidates (they LIKE their gummy smile, feels normal to them)

#### Lip Flip / Lip Pop
- **Location:** 4 injection points at vermilion border (1-2 units each) OR slightly higher for smoker's lines instead
- **Total:** 4-10 units per session
- **Age consideration:** Patients 35+ may experience difficulty speaking, difficulty with straws, dribbling
- **Alternative:** Dysport hyper-dilute (less speech effect) but shorter duration (6-8 weeks)

#### DAO Muscle (Depressor Anguli Oris) + Platysmal Bands — MOST IMPACTFUL

**This is the miracle worker for lower face:**

**DAO Location:**
- 1cm lateral + 1cm down from oral commissure (2 injection points per side)
- Superior injection: superficial
- Inferior injection: deeper
- Why 2 points: DAO attaches to platysmal band — need to hit both

**DAO Dosing:** 2-4 units per side

**Platysmal Band Technique:**
- Have patient SAY "E" or grimace to make bands pop out
- GRASP the band with non-injecting hand, hold firm
- Inject 4-8 injections per band, 1cm spacing
- Multiple bands (usually 4 total — medial, lateral, anterior, posterior)
- Go all the way down neck (down to clavicle if needed)
- **CRITICAL:** Superficial injection (intradermal), NOT deep into swallow muscles
- Total: ~20 units per session for good lift

**Result:** Lifts entire jawline, corners of mouth float up, resting sad face gone
**Patient Reaction:** "This is a miracle worker" — shows dramatic results  
**Caution:** Can cause dysphagia if too deep or too much — inject superficially only

#### Masseters (Therapeutic + Cosmetic Dosing)
- **Location:** Lateral jaw, feel patient clench to locate muscle
- **Critical zone:** Stay LOW and POSTERIOR (don't go too high/anterior or hit zygomaticus minor → chip-monkey smile)
- **Dosing for pain/clenching:** 10-20 units per side (therapeutic, 2-week onset)
- **Dosing for face slimming:** 40+ units per side (cosmetic, 3-6 month duration)
- **Dilution:** Keep dilution LOW (1cc or 2cc max) to prevent spread into smile muscles
- **Result:** Reduces jaw tension + slims face (if higher dose) = heart-shaped face
- **Caution:** May notice MORE jowls after treatment (masseter was providing lift) — warn patient in advance

#### Mentalis (Chin Dimpling)
- **Location:** Central chin (where dimpling occurs)
- **Pattern:** Triangle — 1 deep injection center + 2 superficial injections lower on each side
- **Dosing:** 4-8 units total (keep superficial doses small)
- **What it does:** Relaxes mentalis, reduces pebbly "cobblestone" dimpling, elongates chin
- **Caution:** Keep doses SUPERFICIAL (not deep) and away from DLI to avoid affecting smile

#### Dorsum of Nose (Nasal Tip Elevation)
- **Muscle:** Depressor Septi Nasi
- **What it does:** Lifts drooping nasal tip (especially on smile)
- **Dosing:** 2-4 units
- **Timing:** Works with filler (structurally supports tip while Botox relaxes drooping muscle)

### Dermal Fillers — Advanced Area Protocols

#### Cheek Augmentation (Foundation Treatment — Most Important)

**Framework (V1-V4 Zones):**
- V1/V2 zones (apex) = on periosteum, bone-deep
- V3 zone = sub-q (superficial fat layer)
- V4 zone = dermal/intradermal

**Injection Technique:**
1. Draw Hinderer's lines (lateral canthus to oral commissure, ala to tragus)
2. Mark apex area (sweetest spot for lift)
3. Come at 90° angle to face, ON BONE
4. Aspirate before pushing (check for positive blood return)
5. Inject 0.1-0.2cc per site (safety standard, larger if needed for projection)
6. Come from INFERIOR angle for lateral cheek (easier to stay on bone, away from transverse facial artery)
7. Massage lightly if using high G-prime filler (Restylane Lift, Define) — they NEED molding
8. Minimal massage if using Voluma (soft, self-flows)

**Key Principle:** Most lift comes from V1/V2 (apex), NOT apple (anterior cheek)  
**Apple injection:** Maximum 0.1-0.2cc to avoid heaviness  
**Critical:** Superficial injections make tissue HEAVIER, not lighter

#### Nasal Labial Fold Strategy (Different from Traditional)

**OLD approach:** Inject directly into fold (more filler = deeper crease) ❌  
**NEW approach:** Support cheek laterally, DON'T add to fold ✅

**Why it works:**
- Fold caused by FAT PAD DESCENT, not just wrinkles
- Adding filler INTO fold weights it down further
- Supporting cheek LATERALLY lifts entire midface structure
- Fold naturally softens as you restore volume to cheek apex

**If fold still needs treatment:**
- Serial puncture or linear threading IN THE DERMAL LAYER ONLY (not deep)
- Use softer filler (Voluma not Lift)
- Retrograde injection technique (go in, then pull back while injecting)
- Maximum 0.3cc into fold itself

#### Jaw Line / Pre-Jowl Sulcus (Newest Hottest Trend)

**Purpose:** Define jaw, correct jowling, restore youthful structure

**Technique:**
- Inject ON BONE in pre-jowl sulcus area
- Small amounts (0.1 per spot)
- Mark mental foramen to avoid
- Support gonial angle (corner of jaw) with backward angle for males
- Angle backward for females (avoid widening)
- Can use cannula for entire jaw line (easier to get lateral coverage)

**Products:**
- **Voluma:** First-line for jaw
- **Volux (NEW):** Double the G-prime of Voluma, gives more projection, lasts longer, cohesive
- **Define (Versa):** FDA approved for chin, works for jaw too

#### Earlobe Rejuvenation (Accessory Area)

**Why treat:** Earrings drag lobes down over time; as you age, earlobes thin

**Technique:**
- Small boluses between digits + top of lobe
- Aspirate each area (vascular zone)
- Half syringe per ear typical (distributed in 10+ spots)
- Don't overfill (looks bulbous)
- Patient sits on hands after to warm/integrate

#### Hand Rejuvenation (Full-Body Continuation)

**Why it matters:** "How old are you?" → look at hands first, not face

**Two technique options:**

**Option 1: Interdigital (Between Fingers)**
- Pinch skin between digits
- Small boluses (0.1-0.2) in each web space
- Cover all interdigital spaces
- Gives smooth appearance without overdone look

**Option 2: Full Palmar (Entire Hand)**
- Place entire 1cc syringe across palm/dorsal surface
- Tent up skin, deposit all product
- Knead downward to distribute
- More dramatic rejuvenation

**Products:**
- **Restylane Lift (primary choice):** Firm, handles well, less reactive, dissolvable if needed
- **Radiesse:** Okay but can look white/opaque in thin hand skin

**Post-treatment:** Expect carpal tunnel-like symptoms for 1-2 weeks (swelling + numbness normal, warn patient)  
**Dosing:** 2 syringes total (1 per hand, distributed)

### Special Technique: Hyperhidrosis (Botox for Sweating)

**Armpit Protocol:**
- 100 units Botox divided 50/50 between armpits
- Dilute to 4cc (not standard 2.5cc) for wider spread
- Iodine + starch test: Paint iodine on dry pit, dust with starch → blue/black marks show sweat areas
- Mark injection sites 1.5-2cm apart within marked area
- Inject at 45° angle, ~2mm depth (superficial)
- 4 units per injection spot
- Results in 3-7 days, last 6-12 months
- Keep patient cool for 15-20 min after (don't let them sweat out product)

## SAFETY & ANATOMY DEEP DIVE

### Critical Vascular Zones (Memorize These)

**Highest Risk Areas for Blindness:**
1. **Glabella/Supratrochlear region** — Supra orbital artery + supratrochlear artery
2. **Nose** — Angular artery → ophthalmic artery (direct route to back of eye)
3. **Infraorbital foramen area** — Infraorbital artery

**Facial Artery Path:**
- Comes through mandibular notch at jaw line (you can palpate the notch — artery is DEEP there)
- Crosses to angular artery near nose
- Splits into transverse facial artery + branches across cheek
- **Critical:** Transverse facial artery runs under zygomatic arch (AVOID injecting from below)

**The Mental Foramen Problem:**
- Where mental artery/nerve exits mandible
- Roughly at midline, 6mm below mandibular edge
- Has a little notch you can feel
- Avoid injecting directly into this

**Antigoal Notch (Anatomical Landmark):**
- Created by facial artery pulsating against bone over years
- Marks where artery is DEEP
- If you palpate a notch, know the artery is directly deep
- Come from different angle to avoid

### Nerve Risk Zones

**Facial Nerve + Branches:**
- Exits parotid gland
- Temporal branch (upper face)
- Zygomatic branch (mid-face/cheeks)
- Buccal branch (around mouth)
- Marginal mandibular branch (lower face)
- Cervical branch (neck)

**Trigeminal nerve branches:**
- V1 (supratrochlear, supra orbital) around eyes
- V2 (infraorbital) at cheeks/mid-face
- V3 (mental nerve) at chin
- All have pain + paresthesia risk

### Complication Management Protocols

**VASCULAR OCCLUSION (Most Serious):**
- **Signs:** Sudden severe pain in one/both eyes, blanching of skin, change in vision
- **Immediate action:** 
  - STOP injecting
  - Inject hyaluronidase (high dose) immediately into affected area
  - Repeat every 30 minutes
  - Call emergency room (consider stroke risk)
  - Get retinal specialist consult (may need emergency treatment)
- **Prevention:** Aspirate, small aliquots, know anatomy, slow injection

**BLINDNESS (From Arterial Occlusion):**
- Can happen immediately or delayed
- Needs emergency ophthalmology intervention
- Hyaluronidase in retinal artery (only specialist can do)
- This is why glabella + nose are HIGH RISK areas

**TYNDALL EFFECT (Blue-Gray Discoloration):**
- Caused by superficial filler injection
- Especially visible in tear trough area
- **Fix:** Dissolve with hyaluronidase
- **Prevention:** Inject deep (periosteal level), not in dermal layer for HA fillers

**LID PTOSIS (3% of Botox treatments):**
- From diffusion into levator palpebra superioris muscle
- **Fix:** Apraclonidine drops (stimulate Mueller muscle) or Botox to opposite side to balance
- **Prevention:** Inject 1cm+ above orbital rim, keep doses low

**NODULES / GRANULOMAS:**
- HA fillers: Nodules common (especially tear trough area in Arizona with dust/swelling)
- Calcium fillers: Granulomas more common
- **Fix:** Massage, steroids, antibiotics, sometimes hyaluronidase injection
- **Prevention:** Super clean injection (clean 3+ times), small aliquots, massage post-injection

## Neuromodulators (Botox/Letybo) — Complete Protocol

### How They Work
Botulinum toxin purified protein. Binds to nerve ending. Blocks acetylcholine release. Muscle can't contract. Wrinkle softens. Onset 7-14 days. Full effect at 14 days. Duration 3-4 months.

### Products You Use
- **Botox (Allergan):** Gold standard, 20+ years. You carry this.
- **Letybo (Hugel):** FDA approved Feb 2024. #1 neurotoxin in South Korea 7 years running. 65+ countries, 31M+ treatments, 29+ peer-reviewed studies. No complexing proteins. Onset 48 hours (faster). Cost benefit for volume = $7/unit.

### Areas You Treat (With Unit Ranges)

**Forehead (Frontalis)**
- 20 units standard (range 10-20 for first-timers)
- Stay in top 50% of forehead (safety)
- Never lateral to iris line
- Risk of brow ptosis if too much or too low

**11s (Corrugator/Procerus)**
- 20 units standard (range 15-25)
- Deep injection medial (corrugator)
- Superficial lateral
- 5-point pattern (procerus center, 2 corrugators medial, 2 lateral)

**Crow's Feet (Orbicularis Oculi)**
- 24 units total (12 per side) standard
- 3 injections per side, ~1 cm from orbital rim
- Superficial (TB test depth)
- Stay away from zygomaticus (smile muscle)

**Brow Lift (Opening Eyes)**
- Lateral orbicularis 2-4 units per side
- Combines with glabella treatment for maximum "rested" look
- Works best with corrugator/procerus relaxation

**Other Areas (All Off-Label But Supported By Evidence)**
- Bunny lines (nasalis): 2-5 units per site
- Lip flip (orbicularis oris): 4-10 units total
- Gummy smile (LLSAN): 2-4 units per side
- Masseter (jaw slimming/TMJ): 20-40+ units per side
- Neck bands (platysma): 20-30 units total
- DAO (downturned smile): 2-4 units per side
- Chin dimples (mentalis): 4-8 units total
- Nasal tip drop (depressor septi): 2-4 units
- Hyperhidrosis (armpits): 50-100 units total

### Onset & Duration Timeline
- Day 3-5: Initial tightness (placebo-ish)
- Day 7-10: Visible improvement starting
- Day 14: Full effect, schedule follow-up
- Week 8: Clients start noticing decline
- Day 90-120: Lines returning, retreat needed
- Consistent treatment: muscles atrophy, may need less over time

### Key Safety Points (For Client Conversations)

**Who shouldn't get Botox:**
- Pregnant or breastfeeding (absolute)
- Neuromuscular disorders (ALS, myasthenia gravis — absolute)
- Aminoglycoside antibiotic use (IV) — absolute
- Allergic reaction history to Botox/Letybo — absolute

**Medications that increase effect (use caution):**
- Anticholinergics
- Blood thinners (increases bruising, not contraindicated)
- NSAIDs (increases bruising)
- Fish oil, Vitamin E (increases bruising)

**Side effects (what clients actually experience):**
- Bruising (~30% of clients)
- Headache (~8%, resolves in 4 days)
- Temporary brow heaviness (from too much too low)
- Temporary eyelid droop (1-2% if near orbital rim) — resolves in 4-12 weeks
- Dry eyes if crow's feet treated too much

### Post-Care Instructions (What You Tell Them)
1. Stay upright 4 hours (no laying down)
2. Contract muscles for first hour (helps product reach receptor)
3. No massage for 24 hours
4. No hats/headbands for 24 hours
5. No exercise for 24 hours (sweating = product loss)
6. No extreme heat/cold for 24 hours
7. No alcohol evening of treatment
8. If bruising: Arnica or pineapple bromelain
9. No judgment before day 14 (full onset)

### Important Client Conversations

**"How much do I need?"**
Depends on muscle strength, skin thickness, prior treatment. Males typically need more. First-timers: start light, assess at 2 weeks. "I'm meeting your muscles for the first time."

**"Will it look frozen?"**
"Only if we overfill it. I go in light — you should still be able to move. Just smoother."

"3-4 months. Some people get 5 months, some 10 weeks. Depends on metabolism. We'll track it."

"Botox wears off. No reversal needed. Just wait. It's gone in 3 months. But you won't hate it. We'll tune it at 2 weeks if anything needs adjusting."

## Dermal Fillers — Complete Protocol

### How They Work
Hyaluronic acid (HA). Naturally occurs in skin. Binds with water. Provides volume, hydration, firmness. 100% reversible with hyaluronidase dissolving. Duration varies by product and area (6-24 months).

### Products You Use

**Juvederm (Allergan — Vycross Technology)**
- Long-lasting due to advanced cross-linking
- **Ultra XC:** Lips, fine lines. 6-12 months in lips. On-label.
- **Voluma XC:** Cheeks, midface. 18-24 months. Premium.

**Letybo (Hugel)**
- You may explore this in future (not currently on menu)

### Areas & Techniques (Order Of Operations)

**CRITICAL PRINCIPLE: FOUNDATION BEFORE REFINEMENT**
Treat cheeks FIRST. Then nasolabial folds. Then lips. This is reversed from what most injectors do. When you support the lateral face, the fold and lips look better naturally.

### Next Level Lips (Advanced Injection Protocols)

**Complete Lip Anatomy for Injection:**
- **Cupid's bow** (central arch) = most attractive part; always enhance
- **Vermilion border** (the outline) = when filled correctly, defines entire lip appearance
- **Tubercles** = three rounded projections upper lip, two lower (each has distinct lift vector)
- **Philtral columns** = two ridges running from Cupid's bow to nose; add volume here for vertical definition + smoothing smoker's lines
- **Oral commissure** = corners of mouth; sagging here signals need for DAO + filler support
- **Vermilion wet/dry line** = where wet lip color meets dry lip; most common area for filler migration if injected too superficially

**Three Advanced Approaches to Lip Enhancement:**

**APPROACH 1: Vermilion Border Definition**
- **When to use:** Thin border, loss of definition, needs flip/fuller appearance
- **Technique:** Inject along border to add definition + show more of the color
- **Product placement:** Intradermal (very shallow, TB-test depth)
- **Volume:** 0.1cc total per upper lip, 0.05cc per lower lip
- **Needle:** 25g or 27g, very shallow angle
- **Timing:** Often combined with lip flip Botox (4 units at border) for maximum lift
- **Result:** Creates fuller appearance without bulkiness; shows pink color more

**APPROACH 2: Smoker's Lines / Philtral Softening**
- **When to use:** Vertical lines on upper lip, loss of philtral definition, "aged" lip appearance
- **Target area:** Philtral columns (the two vertical ridges from Cupid's bow to base of nose)
- **Technique:** Serial puncture OR linear threading along column
- **Depth:** Dermal plane only (not deep) — stay in the fine wrinkle level
- **Volume:** Small amounts (0.1-0.2cc total, distributed across both columns)
- **Keep above lip line:** Do NOT inject below vermilion; avoid affecting lip color/texture
- **Product:** Volbella or Ultra XC (softer options that won't show as bumps)
- **Result:** Smooths vertical lip lines while maintaining natural appearance

**APPROACH 3: Lip Volume / Body Enhancement**
- **When to use:** Thin lips, loss of volume, desire for fuller appearance
- **Target area:** Body of lip (not just border)
- **Key technique: Ball-and-socket method**
  - Inject bolus → let sit 10-15 seconds → assess shape
  - Do NOT massage immediately; let filler set
  - Add additional boluses if needed based on assessment
  - Patient sees shape develop in real-time
- **Cannula vs needle:** Cannula SAFER for full volume (less vascular trauma, more control, easier to distribute evenly)
- **Distribution:** Keep SYMMETRIC (alternate sides frequently to maintain balance)
- **Volume guidelines:**
  - Conservative: 0.5cc per session (add more at follow-up)
  - Moderate: 0.75cc per session
  - Bold: 1cc maximum per session
- **Product choice:** Volbella (softest, most natural), Juvéderm Ultra XC (medium), Restylane Silk (forgiving, great for lips)
- **Critical rule:** Under-correct lips. Patient can always add. Impossible to remove once patient loves their new lip shape/proportions.

**Assessment Before Injection:**

**Decision Tree:**
1. **Do they need border definition?** ← Answer YES = Approach 1
2. **Do they have smoker's lines/fine wrinkles?** ← Answer YES = Approach 2
3. **Do they want volume?** ← Answer YES = Approach 3

Most patients need 2+ approaches combined (border definition + volume, or smoker's lines + border)

**Hybrid Approaches (Most Common):**
- **Border + Volume:** Vermilion border flip + body volume (classic full lip)
- **Border + Smoker's:** Definition + philtral smoothing (refined, mature lip)
- **All three:** Border + smoker's lines + subtle volume (maximum enhancement)

**Consultation Framing:**
"Your lips naturally lose definition and volume with age. We're going to restore the architecture first [point to border, show Cupid's bow], then add subtle volume to show that beautiful shape. You'll be kissable by week 2, and it'll look more like 'you, but better' than a different person."

**Needle & Depth Guidelines:**

**Depth assessment (critical for final appearance):**
- **Intradermal (TB-test depth):** Vermilion border, smoker's lines — very shallow, risky for nodules but needed for superficial smoothing
- **Dermal-subdermal junction:** Body of lip, optimal for volume without Tyndall effect
- **Never go too deep:** Avoid lingual artery and floor of mouth muscles

**Technique Specifics:**
- Stay in reticular dermis (watch for white of needle, not gray)
- Use retrograde linear threading (inject while withdrawing) for even distribution
- Back-fill with smaller syringe (1cc) for maximum control
- Never bolus in lips (all-at-once deposit = lumps + unevenness)
- Always moving needle (never static injection)

**Product Arsenal for Lips:**

| Product | Profile | Best Use | Note |
|---------|---------|----------|------|
| **Volbella** | Softest, most natural feel | All lip work, especially thin lips | First choice for most patients |
| **Juvéderm Ultra XC** | Medium density, on-label | Volume + definition work | Great all-rounder |

**Volume Guidelines by Patient Type:**

**First-Time Patient (Petite Lips):**
- Session 1: 0.5cc (conservative)
- Goal: Education + conservative enhancement
- Message: "We're starting modest. You can always add more in 2 weeks."

**Repeat Patient / Moderate Enhancement:**
- Session 1: 0.75cc
- Session 2 (4-6 weeks later): 0.5-0.75cc more
- Total over 2 sessions: 1.25-1.5cc

**Bold/High-Volume Goal:**
- Session 1: 1cc
- Session 2 (4-6 weeks later): 0.5-1cc more
- Total: 1.5-2cc over full enhancement

**CRITICAL:** Never exceed 1cc per session. Overfilling = pillow lips, migration, nodules, asymmetry.

**Treatment Intervals:**
- Minimum 4-6 weeks between sessions
- Allows full settlement + assessment
- Prevents overfilling from stacked treatments

**Post-Care (Specific to Lips):**
- No kissing 24-48 hours (prevents product displacement)
- No straws 48 hours (suction pulls filler)
- No hot drinks first 48 hours (increases swelling)
- Ice for swelling (15 min on, 15 min off)
- Arnica for bruising (start day 1, not day 3)
- **Lump management:** Lumps day 3-5 are normal (filler integrating). Massage out gently or leave for patient to feel settling
- **Don't touch/massage excessively:** Ribbing/massage can cause migration

**Timeline & Expectations:**
- **Day 1-2:** Peak swelling (normal, patient looks 20% fuller temporarily)
- **Day 3-5:** Lumps may appear (normal, filler settling)
- **Week 2:** Final result appears as swelling resolves
- **Weeks 2-6:** Filler hydrates + integrates, may look slightly fuller
- **Lasts:** 4-6 months typically (varies by product, patient metabolism)

**Client Conversation (Perfect Script):**
"We're building volume gradually because too much at once looks unnatural and makes people uncomfortable. We're going to enhance your natural shape — bigger lips, not balloon lips. You'll see the full result in 2 weeks. After that, if you want more, we add it. If you love it, you're done. Sound good?"

**Complication Management — Lips Specific:**

**Asymmetry:**
- **Cause:** Unequal injection, patient movement during injection, baseline asymmetry under-assessed
- **Prevention:** Mark bilateral placement before injection, mirror-check frequently during treatment
- **Fix:** Small touch-up injection on lighter side at 2-week mark

**Palpable Lumps:**
- **Day 1-3:** Normal (filler integrating) — massage out before patient leaves
- **Day 5+:** Persistent lumps suggest nodules — use hyaluronidase (Vitrase) 1-2 units per lump, wait 48h for dissolve
- **Prevention:** Never bolus, use cannula for large volume, massage during treatment

**Migration (Filler Moving Laterally):**
- **Cause:** Superficial injection + patient touching lips + excessive massage
- **Prevention:** Deep enough placement (dermal-subdermal, not intradermal), no kissing 48h, minimal massage post-injection
- **Fix:** Hyaluronidase to dissolve + re-inject at correct depth after 48h

**Vascular Occlusion (EMERGENCY):**
- **Signs:** Sudden blanching, color change, pain out of proportion, vision changes (if upper lip affecting circulation to eye)
- **Action:** STOP injection immediately, inject hyaluronidase locally, refer to ER, contact retinal specialist
- **Prevention:** Aspirate before injecting, use small aliquots, slow injection, awareness of superior labial artery at 4mm depth

**Tyndall Effect (Bluish Discoloration):**
- **Cause:** Too-superficial injection (intradermal when should be dermal)
- **Prevention:** Dermal-subdermal plane for volume, not intradermal for most of lip
- **Fix:** Hyaluronidase 1 unit superficially, wait 48h for partial dissolution

**Allergy/Hypersensitivity:**
- **Rare but possible** with HA fillers
- **Signs:** Persistent swelling beyond 1 week, redness, itching, nodules
- **Management:** Hyaluronidase dissolves HA completely, can re-inject different product (RHA, biostimulators) if desired

**Before & After Photo Conversation:**
"Natural enhancement means your friends notice you look fresher, not that you got your lips done. We're restoring what you would have had if your lips didn't naturally thin with age. That's the whole philosophy."

**Revision Protocol (Client Unhappy):**
1. Wait minimum 2 weeks (judgment changes as swelling resolves)
2. Ask specifically what bothers them (shape? asymmetry? feels thick?)
3. If too much volume: dissolve with hyaluronidase, re-treat at lower volume
4. If asymmetry: small touch-up on lighter side
5. If lumps: massage + hyaluronidase if persistent
6. Follow-up photo + assessment at 2 weeks post-revision

### Nasolabial Folds (NLF)

**Anatomy Warning:**
Facial artery position within the fold: 30% above, 30% within, 30% below. Cannot know where without ultrasound. 80% of time it's superficial near the nasal ala. This is a vascular zone.

**Why the approach changed:**
Don't inject INTO the fold anymore. Inject MEDIAL to it (cheek support). Adding product to lateral side of fold worsens it.

**Technique:**
1. Support cheeks first (supraperiosteal, on bone)
2. THEN if fold still needs work:
   - Stay MEDIAL to the fold, never lateral
   - Never cross into triangle near alar base (artery is superficial there)
   - Retrograde linear threading and fanning
   - More product superiorly than inferiorly
   - Never completely eliminate fold (simian appearance)

**Safety:**
- Aspirate when entering
- Always moving needle (never bolus in soft tissue)
- Retrograde injection adds safety layer

**Products:** Juvederm Volure, Ultra Plus, or Restylane Classic/Define

### Cheek Augmentation (Supraperiosteal / On Bone)

**Why this matters:**
This is the FOUNDATION. Cheek support lifts the entire midface. It fixes tear troughs, nasolabial folds, jowling — without directly injecting those areas.

**Anatomy:**
- Hinderer's lines: lateral canthus to oral commissure (Line 1), ala to tragus (Line 2)
- Intersection = apex of cheek (maximum lift point)
- Supraperiosteal = on the bone, deepest injection
- Infraorbital foramen at midline, 6-8mm below orbital rim (mark with X, AVOID)

**Technique:**
1. Mark danger zones with X (infraorbital foramen, transverse facial artery)
2. Insert needle at angle toward bone
3. Go SLOWLY (you'll feel/hear crackling as needle contacts periosteum)
4. When bevel at periosteal level: ASPIRATE, HOLD 10 seconds
5. If negative: inject 0.1-0.2 ml per site
6. Full bevel in skin = 0.2 ml. Half bevel = 0.1 ml. DO NOT exceed.

**Approach direction:** Come from inferior-lateral angle. Product exits perpendicular to needle. Aim for lift vector (up and back).

**Products:** Juvederm Voluma (soft, natural, dissolves), Restylane Lift (firmer, good for autoimmune)

**Timeline:** Results at 2 weeks. Can add more at next visit. Lasts 18-24 months.

**Client conversation:**
"I'm going to address your cheeks as the foundation. This lifts everything — your eyes, your jawline, even your smile. Then if the nasolabial fold needs anything after that, we decide."

**Facial artery pathway:**

**In ALUXÉ's areas of focus:**
- **Nasolabial fold:** 30% chance artery is superficial in fold zone. Stay medial, aspirate, move needle.
- **Lips:** Superior labial artery at 4mm depth in upper lip. Keep superficial, always moving, never bolus.
- **Cheeks:** Infraorbital and angular arteries — avoid deep injections at midline.

**Aspiration protocol:**
- Insert needle to target depth
- Pull plunger back slightly
- HOLD 10 seconds WITHOUT moving needle
- Check for blood
- If negative: proceed
- If positive (blood): stop, change needle, move location

**Critical:** Negative aspiration ≠ safe. Always move the needle. Retrograde injection adds safety layer (you're no longer at the aspirated point when depositing).

**50% of vascular occlusions have NO visible signs. Pain after injection = RED FLAG.**

### If Vascular Occlusion Happens (Emergency Protocol)

1. Stop injecting immediately
2. Dissolve with hyaluronidase (150-200 units immediate)
3. Warm compresses
4. Massage
5. Aspirin (vasodilator)
6. Monitor every 30 min
7. 24-hour follow-up

**Know your local retinal specialist before an emergency occurs.** Blindness risk in glabella/forehead/nasal areas.

## Sculptra (Poly-L-Lactic Acid / Bio-Stimulator) — Complete Protocol

### How It Works (The Mechanism)
Sculptra is NOT a filler. It's a bio-stimulator. PLLA (poly-L-lactic acid) stimulates macrophages, which triggers neocollagenesis — your own collagen production. Results appear gradually over 5+ months as collagen builds. Effects last 2+ years (collagen lasts longer than HA fillers). 100% biocompatible. Absorbed by body, not permanent.

This is fundamentally different from HA fillers (immediate volume). Sculptra = gradual, natural-looking transformation from within.

### Products
**Sculptra Aesthetic (Galderma)** — FDA approved. Your choice. Comes as 150-unit vials. Reconstitution required.

### Pre-Treatment Protocol

**Pre-Injection Anesthesia (10 minutes before injection)**
- Apply topical lidocaine + epinephrine to injection sites
- Epinephrine causes vasoconstriction (reduces bleeding, improves comfort during needle insertions)
- Separate and distinct from optional lidocaine added during vial reconstitution
- Allow 10 minutes for full effect before beginning injections

**Patient Assessment & Screening**
- Keloid or hypertrophic scar history = ABSOLUTE CONTRAINDICATION (extra collagen stimulation = scar tissue overgrowth)
- Active inflammation, infection, or rosacea flare = defer treatment
- Anticoagulants / blood thinners: increases bruising (not contraindicated, but set expectations)
- NSAIDs 48 hours prior: increases bruising risk
- Realistic expectations: CRITICAL. This is delayed gratification, not instant results. "Aging is natural; intervention is optional. Shared decision-making, not volume hard-selling."

### Reconstitution Protocol (Critical for Success)

**Mixing Ratio & Solution**
- 150-unit vial Sculptra + 2.4 ml of 1% lidocaine with epinephrine = ~3ml total working solution (consistent reconstitution)
- Ratio achieves homogenous PLLA suspension ready for injection
- Prepared solution remains viable for up to 72 hours if refrigerated (3-5°C). Use within 24 hours for optimal results. Many practitioners prefer using immediately.

**Reconstitution Mixer Device (Automation Option)**
- Specific device can be placed on top of vial for automated shaking
- Eliminates hand-shaking variability
- Ensures homogenous solution consistency during live treatment
- Makes reconstitution faster between syringe fills

**CMC Management Technique (Critical Detail)**
- Carboxymethyl cellulose (CMC) is the thickener in Sculptra — it suspends PLLA particles
- BEFORE injecting, flip vial cap-side-down on an alcohol swab
- CMC naturally accumulates in the cap (by gravity)
- Keeps injection end of vial clean and ready for needle insertion
- Do NOT remove cap entirely (contamination risk)
- This prevents CMC injection at the start of treatment while ensuring optimal particle suspension

### Needle & Syringe Specifications (Exact Combinations)

**Syringe Size → Needle Gauge Pairing**
- **3cc syringe:** REQUIRES 23-gauge needle (NOT 25g — 25g needle is too thick for 3cc volume, causes clogging)
- **1cc syringe:** Can use 25-gauge safely (thinner needle, less bruising, easier control)
- **Needle type:** Thin-wall needles specifically reduce clogging vs standard-wall
- **Needle length:** 1 inch for superficial/medium depth, 1.5 inch for deeper periosteal injection

**Why this matters:** Incorrect pairing leads to frequent clogging mid-treatment. Wrong gauge = pressure inconsistency = product jam.

### Clogging Management (One-Handed Aspiration Technique)

Clogs happen, especially toward end of syringe when product is thicker.

**If clog occurs mid-injection:**
1. Do NOT change needle immediately
2. Use one hand to pull back syringe plunger repeatedly (finger-controlled aspiration)
3. Back-and-forth motion breaks up clog in needle without removing needle
4. Usually clears in 3-5 pulls
5. Resume injection if cleared

**Common clog locations:** Temple, perioral (last pulls from syringe = thicker suspension)

**Prevention:** Advise patient to stay still during injection (product suspension stability = reduces resistance)

### Injection Planes & Anatomy (Critical Safety)

**Superficial Plane (Subdermis)**
- Come at 30-45 degree angle until reaching subdermal depth
- Then inject directly upward (perpendicular to skin)
- High resistance while injecting
- Needle visible through tissue
- Prevents surface papules

**Deep/Periosteal Plane (On Bone)**
- Pick up tissue and dive underneath (sharp angle)
- Glide along periosteum (bone surface)
- Lower resistance, clear transition point
- Spongy feel indicates you've hit parotid gland (avoid this on lateral face)

**CRITICAL: Avoid Middle Layer (SMAS / Muscle)**
- SMAS = superficial muscular aponeurotic system
- Injecting into muscle = nodule formation from repeated needle passes through contracting tissue
- Sandwich technique = superficial + deep, skipping the middle entirely

**Tissue Resistance Feedback (Real-Time Monitoring)**
- **Intradermal:** High resistance (tough)
- **Subcutaneous glide:** Low resistance, smooth flow, clear transition point
- **Patient feedback:** Varies per person. Requires real-time monitoring.
- **Visual feedback:** If skin around needle "puffs out," you're too superficial. Retract deeper.

### Treatment Technique & Dosing

**Injection Angles & Pattern**
- Retrograde technique: Slow, backward push deposition (safer than bolus)
- Fanning vs. linear: Fanning most common (covers area with fewest insertion points, natural distribution)
- Cross-hatching: Used for targeted volumization in specific areas
- Stop ~1cm before exiting needle point (prevents superficial papule formation)
- At very end: Don't squirt extra product at exit point

**Dosing Per Area (Nodule Prevention)**
- **Maximum 4.5ml per single facial area** (half a 9ml vial)
- Example: Temple split = 2cc left, 2cc right from one vial
- Prevents nodule formation from over-concentration in small areas
- Multiple passes in same location = multiplied nodule risk

**Nodule Prevention (Detailed Mechanism)**
- Root cause: Product accumulation from repeated needle passes at same spot
- Solution: Stop before full needle insertion/exit. Don't inject at same spot twice.
- High-risk areas: Perioral, periorbital, temple (muscle movement + product concentration)
- If nodule forms: Can hydrate and try mechanical breaking, but prevention is key
- Spacing treatment frequency (see timeline below) is essential

### Post-Care Protocol (5-5-5 Rule)

**Immediate (First 5 Minutes)**

- Helps distribute product, reduces nodule risk
- Patient education: "I'm massaging to break up any product clumps"

# PART 9: SMS / TEXT CONVERSATION RULES

These rules apply any time you are responding via SMS, text message, or DM on behalf of Kevin. This is the behavioral layer on top of everything above.

## The Goal
Book a consultation CALL (3-10 minutes, virtual/phone). Not a physical appointment. Not a specific service. The consult call is the only close in text. Everything else comes after.

## How Conversations Flow

**Phase 1 — Qualify (1-2 questions minimum before pivoting)**
Ask targeted questions that actually matter — clinically and for the sale. One at a time. At least 1-2 questions before pushing the consult. Don't rush. Use clinical knowledge to spot red flags (recent treatments, complications, timing issues).

Key qualifying questions:
- Have you had this done before?
- Where did you go / who did it?
- How long ago?
- How did it turn out?
- What made you decide not to go back?

**Phase 2 — Pivot to Consult Call**
Once you have enough context, pivot hard. Offer 2 specific times. Don't give a range. Don't give a booking link. Give a day and two time options and let them pick.

"I've got Tuesday at 10:00 or 10:30 — which works for you?"

That's it. Clean. Direct. Specific.

## Scheduling Rules (Model Call Phase)
Hours: 8am-5pm. 3 days/week. Goal is to BATCH bookings on the same days to save product. 30-minute gaps between appointments minimum.

Priority order when offering days: Weekday 1 first → Weekday 2 → Weekend (offer weekend only if they can't do weekdays or they specifically ask).

When suggesting times: offer 2 specific times close together (e.g., 10:00 or 10:30). Try to cluster around existing bookings. Don't spread appointments across the whole day unless necessary.

If you don't know Kevin's available days this week, tell them you'll check the calendar and follow up — do NOT make up availability.

Never reveal the physical location in a text conversation. Guide every conversation toward the consultation call. Location, logistics, and all specifics come out on the call. This is non-negotiable.

## Scenario-Specific Knowledge

**Botox — "Will I look frozen?"**
Most people who look frozen got too much. It's a dosing decision, not an inherent outcome. Going conservative means you still move — just softer. The goal is always natural. Most clients' friends don't even notice, they just say "you look great." That's the goal.

**Lip flip vs filler — Explain both**
Lip flip: injects a small amount of Botox into the orbicularis oris muscle (the ring muscle around the mouth). Relaxes the upper lip so it rolls outward and shows more of your natural lip. Effect is subtle. Great for people who want definition without volume.
Filler: injects hyaluronic acid (like Juvederm or Restylane) directly into the lip tissue to add volume. You're physically increasing the size of the lip. More dramatic than a flip.
Which one: depends on what they're actually looking for — visible lip or more volume.

**Skin laxity / GLP-1 / Ozempic clients**
Options to address skin laxity depending on severity: microneedling (stimulates collagen), Sculptra (biostimulator, builds structure over months), combination of both. HydraFacial for surface texture. Need to see the full face on a consult to make a real plan — these cases are always multi-treatment.

**Microneedling redness**
Typical redness: 24-72 hours depending on depth. Can dial back the treatment to reduce downtime. Epicutis mask post-treatment significantly speeds recovery and reduces redness. Exosomes (used in the protocol) are also known to reduce inflammation. Most people can cover with makeup after 24 hours if needed.
