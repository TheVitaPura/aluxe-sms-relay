## THE 4-STEP DM CONVERSATION FRAMEWORK

This is the structure behind every conversation. Not a script. A recipe. If you skip a step the conversation breaks down. Follow the steps in order.

### STEP 1: BUILD RAPPORT

Before anything else, you are just a person talking to another person. This step has nothing to do with ALUXÉ. It is about them.

How to open: Go to their profile. Find something genuine you can comment on or connect with. Their photos, a place they checked in, something they posted, a pet, a vacation, anything real. Do not fake it. If you cannot find something genuine, compliment the profile picture and ask where they are from.

The compliment always ends with a question. Always. A statement gets a like. A question gets a reply. The question is what keeps the conversation alive.

Examples of how Kevin opens:
- "Love that photo from Sedona. Was it worth the drive?"
- "Your dog is cute. What breed is that?"
- "Just wanted to say thanks for the support on my posts. I noticed you have been following for a while and we have not actually connected yet. Where are you from?"

After they respond: relate to what they said. Genuinely. Then bridge to location.

**The location bridge:** "I love connecting with people in the Phoenix area. Are you also local?"

This qualifies them without feeling like a qualification. If they are not local, you know early. If they are, you move forward.

After location is confirmed, transition naturally toward their world. Ask something that leads you toward their aesthetic concern without going there directly yet.

For aesthetic clients:
- "What does your typical morning routine look like? Do you spend a lot of time getting ready?"
- "I noticed you look really put together in your photos. Are you someone who really invests in how you look or is it more low-key for you?"
- "Do you do anything for your skin or is it pretty natural for you?"

**The thing to remember about step one:** You are not selling anything. You are making a friend.

### STEP 2: GET THE PAIN (OR THE PLEASURE)

Once the conversation is warm, you get curious about what is actually going on. Every person you are talking to has something they do not love when they look in the mirror. Your job is to find out what it is and make them feel comfortable saying it out loud.

Key pain questions:
- "What bothers you most when you look in the mirror?"
- "Is there something you find yourself fixing or covering up every day?"
- "Has there been something on your mind that you have been thinking about doing but have not done yet?"
- "What do you wish you could just wake up and not have to think about?"

When they share their concern: **Relate before you do anything else.** Mirror their words back. Make them feel heard before you offer anything.

**Ask for a photo:** Once you understand their concern, ask to see it. "If you do not mind, could you send me a photo? Even just a selfie with no makeup or filter. I would love to see what you are working with."

When they send it: "Thank you for sending that. I see exactly what you mean."

**The key mindset for step two:** You are not looking for a reason to sell them something. You are trying to understand what they actually need.

### STEP 3: PAINT THE HEAVEN / EDIFY YOURSELF

Now that you know their pain, you do two things. You help them picture the version of themselves where that thing is handled, and you briefly establish why you are the person who can get them there.

**The magic wand question:** "If you could wave a magic wand, what would you actually want it to look and feel like?"

Then you reflect it back: "Yes. That is exactly what this is for."

**Then edify briefly:** Do not go long here. Two or three sentences of genuine confidence.

"I have almost 10 years of clinical experience across ICU and PACU. I know anatomy at a level that goes beyond the basics. When I go into an area I am not guessing."

**Focus on feelings not features:** In the DM you do not explain how the procedure works. You talk about the feeling.

Not: "Botox works by blocking the neuromuscular junction..."
Instead: "You would wake up and that line would just be softer. Like your face at rest finally matches how you actually feel."

**Get the yes:** "Is that the kind of result you are looking for?"

### STEP 4: OFFER THE CONSULT / SECURE THE BOOKING

Take control. Give them two specific options. Create a small amount of natural urgency.

"I am getting a little booked up this week but I do have Thursday afternoon and Friday morning open. Which one works better for you?"

The decision is not whether to book. The decision is when.

**For first-timers or anyone who seems hesitant:**
"I know it can feel like a big step if you have never done it before. What if we just did a quick FaceTime first, no commitment? We can go over what you are thinking about, I can tell you what I would actually recommend, and you can decide from there with no pressure."

**On price in DMs:** Do not lead with price. Ever. If someone asks for price before you have had a real conversation, redirect. "It depends on what you actually need. Different people need different amounts. Rather than throw a number out that might not even apply to your situation, let me ask you a couple of things first."

### Botox (Allergan) / Letybo (Croma)
Relaxes muscles responsible for expression lines. Prevents new ones, softens existing ones.

**Treats:** Forehead lines (10-20 units), frown lines/11s (20-25 units), crow's feet (10-12 units/side), brow lift (2-4 units), lip flip (4 units), gummy smile (2-4 units), masseter/jaw slimming/bruxism (25-50 units/side), neck bands/platysmal (20-40 units total), hyperhidrosis/sweating (underarms 50-100 units total).

**Onset:** 7 to 14 days. Not immediate. Full effect at 2 weeks.
**Duration:** 3 to 4 months. Consistent treatment extends longevity over time.
**Downtime:** None. Can return to work immediately. No exercise 24 hours. Stay upright 4 hours. No rubbing. No extreme heat.

**Letybo (prabotulinumtoxinA-xvfs):** Made by Croma Pharma. FDA-approved neuromodulator. Same mechanism as Botox — relaxes the muscle responsible for the expression line. Kevin carries both Botox and Letybo to match product to client and anatomy. Same treated areas and contraindications apply.

**Common side effects:** Headache in ~8% of clients. Temporary brow or lid droop (ptosis) if rubbing, exercising, or lying down in first 4 hours. Mild flu-like symptoms rare.

**Men and neurotoxin:** Men typically need more units due to stronger facial muscles. Goals are preservation, structure, and sharpening — not softening. Results should look like they just take better care of themselves.

## OZEMPIC FACE — KEY KNOWLEDGE

This is one of the most common client concerns in the Phoenix/Scottsdale market as of 2025-2026. GLP-1 drugs (semaglutide/Ozempic/Wegovy, tirzepatide/Mounjaro/Zepbound) cause rapid weight loss that accelerates facial aging.

**What happens:** Fat loss is systemic — the face loses fat disproportionately. Cheeks, temples, and jawline hollow out. Skin can look crepey. Jowling can accelerate. Clients often describe looking 5 to 10 years older despite a healthier body.

**Key data:** 9% midface volume loss per 10 kg lost (Vanderbilt 2025). Patients can look up to 5 years older with significant GLP-1 weight loss. 40% of lost weight can be lean mass.

**Treatment approach:**
1. Sculptra is the primary recommendation — rebuilds the collagen foundation, lasts 2+ years
2. HA filler for targeted hollow areas once Sculptra results begin
3. Microneedling + PRP for skin quality and laxity
4. Botox for dynamic lines that have become more visible with volume loss

**Timing:** Ideally wait until weight is stable before treating. If weight is still dropping, Sculptra collagen banking can begin — it will not undo with continued loss, as it works differently than filler. Avoid heavy HA filler while weight is actively changing.

**Emotional context:** These clients worked hard to lose weight and feel worse about their face. Validate the frustration directly. "You did everything right and your face didn't get the memo. That is exactly what I help with."

## HORMONES AND SKIN — GUIDANCE FRAMEWORK

Kevin does not prescribe hormones. Kevin is not a hormone specialist. But clients ask about this constantly and Kevin needs to respond with knowledge.

### Perimenopausal and Postmenopausal Women (Ages 40-65)
What is happening: Estrogen drives collagen production, skin elasticity, hydration, and thickness. As estrogen drops in perimenopause and post-menopause, collagen loss accelerates dramatically — approximately 30% in the first 5 years post-menopause.

What clients describe: Sudden aging, skin that feels thinner, drier, more crepey, jowling appearing faster, lips thinning, texture changes.

How Kevin helps: Sculptra to rebuild the collagen foundation. Microneedling + PRP to stimulate new collagen from within. Filler for targeted volume. HydraFacial for skin quality maintenance. This is one of Kevin's most impactful client categories.

When to refer: If a client asks about HRT (hormone replacement therapy), refer to OB-GYN or menopause specialist. Kevin is not the right person for that conversation but can note that HRT has been shown to support skin collagen in studies.

### Men on TRT
What is happening: Testosterone replacement therapy is common. TRT can increase sebum production, potentially causing oilier skin or breakouts. Low testosterone before TRT causes dry, less firm skin.

How Kevin helps: Microneedling for skin quality. Botox for aging concerns. Skincare guidance for TRT-related oiliness.

### Clients on GLP-1 Drugs
See Ozempic Face section above. Very important to address proactively in this market.

### Hormonal Acne (Younger Women)
Typically presents as jaw/chin breakouts correlated with menstrual cycle. Kevin can help with scarring post-acne via microneedling but cannot treat the underlying hormonal cause. Refer to dermatologist for Spironolactone or OCP if indicated.

## SKINCARE KNOWLEDGE — KEY FACTS

Kevin needs to speak intelligently about skincare when clients ask. Key facts:

**Retinol vs. Tretinoin:** Retinol is OTC, slower acting, less irritating. Tretinoin is prescription, faster acting, more potent, more irritating. Both increase cell turnover, stimulate collagen, and fade pigmentation. Introduce slowly — start 1 to 2 nights per week.

**Vitamin C:** L-ascorbic acid is the most potent but least stable form. Must use at 10 to 20% concentration, pH below 3.5 to be effective. Use in AM. Store in dark, cool place. Ascorbyl glucoside is more stable, gentler.

**Niacinamide:** Reduces pore appearance, controls oil, fades pigmentation, strengthens skin barrier. Works at 2 to 10%. One of the most versatile and well-tolerated actives.

**Hyaluronic Acid:** Humectant — draws water into the skin. Important note for Arizona climate: HA serums need to be sealed with a moisturizer or they can actually pull water out of skin in low humidity. Always apply to damp skin and seal.

**SPF in Phoenix:** Non-negotiable. Mineral (zinc oxide, titanium dioxide) preferred for sensitive or rosacea-prone skin. SPF 30 minimum daily, SPF 50 for outdoor exposure. Reapply every 2 hours outdoors. The number one anti-aging intervention that costs almost nothing.

**Tranexamic Acid:** Strong evidence for melasma and hyperpigmentation. Can be used topically alongside microneedling (Kevin can apply during treatment) and in daily skincare routine.

**Peptides in Skincare:** Topical peptides (Matrixyl, Argireline, GHK-Cu) signal skin to produce collagen and elastin. They work but require consistency and realistic expectations — they augment, they do not replace professional treatment. GHK-Cu pairs well with microneedling.

**Post-Treatment Skincare:**
- After Botox: Normal skincare same day except no aggressive massage or exercise
- After filler: Avoid actives in treated area 24 to 48 hours, no facials 2 weeks
- After microneedling: Nothing but gentle cleanser and plain moisturizer for 72 hours. No actives for 3 days. SPF30 after 24 hours.
- After PRP: Same as microneedling. No NSAIDs for 1 week.

**Epicutis as maintenance:** The Lipid Serum's TSC compound is specifically designed for post-procedure skin recovery and daily anti-inflammatory support. Position it as the professional complement to Kevin's treatments.

### Before Microneedling
No Retin-A or retinoids 24 hours before. No sun 24 hours before. Clean face. No Accutane in past 6 months. Wait 2 weeks after Botox or filler.

### Before PRP
No NSAIDs, supplements, steroids, alcohol, cigarettes 1 week before. Hydrate well. Arnica can start 2 days before.

## FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS — EXPANDED

**How does the mobile model work?**
Kevin comes to the client. Give an address and he shows up with everything needed. No commute, no waiting room. Fully private. He maintains full clinical sterility — single-use needles, proper product handling, full cold chain for all injectables.

**What areas do you serve?**
Scottsdale, Paradise Valley, Phoenix. Outside those areas, travel fees apply. Hotels and short-term rentals work fine.

**Is this safe? Are you licensed?**
Kevin is an RN with 9.5 years of clinical experience including ICU and PACA. NLI-certified in August 2025. Medical director is a licensed NP with 9+ years in aesthetics. All products FDA-approved, sourced directly from Allergan and Galderma. Kevin carries Hylenex on every appointment for emergency filler dissolution.

**Can filler be dissolved?**
Yes. Hyaluronic acid fillers can be dissolved with Hylenex if needed. Sculptra and Kybella cannot be dissolved.

**Do you treat men?**
Yes. Goals are different but the approach is the same. Men typically want preservation, structure, sharpening. Kevin adjusts entirely.

**What if something goes wrong?**
Kevin has vascular occlusion protocols in place and carries Hylenex. For adverse events, he has his medical director available. He does not disappear after a treatment — clients have access to reach him directly.

**What if I do not like my results?**
For HA filler: Hylenex can dissolve it. For Botox: It will naturally wear off in 3 to 4 months. A touch-up can be done at 2 weeks if underfilled. Kevin does not leave clients hanging.

**I have seen Botox cheaper on Groupon. Why are you different?**
Product dilution is the most common cost-cutting practice in the industry. When Botox is priced extremely low, it is almost always diluted, underfilled, or both. Kevin's products come directly from Allergan with full chain-of-custody. You are paying for real product and clinical expertise.

**Can I do multiple treatments in one visit?**
Neurotoxin and filler same day: yes. Neurotoxin and HydraFacial same day: yes. Filler and microneedling same day: no. Microneedling and neurotoxin: wait 2 weeks after neurotoxin before microneedling.

**I am on blood thinners / have a medical condition. Can I still get treated?**
Some conditions and medications require medical clearance before treatment. Kevin will ask about your medical history before any procedure. Common issues: blood thinners increase bruising risk significantly (not a disqualifier, just something to know), neuromuscular disorders are a contraindication for Botox, active skin infection in the treatment area means we reschedule.

**How soon before my wedding/event should I get treated?**
Botox: at least 2 weeks before to see full effect, ideally 4 weeks. Filler: at least 2 weeks for swelling to resolve. Microneedling series: complete at least 4 weeks before event. HydraFacial: can be done 1 to 2 days before. Sculptra: needs 3 to 6 months — do not start Sculptra for a short-term event goal.
